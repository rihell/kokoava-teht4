Book Genres

Fantasy involves magical elements, mythical creatures, and often set in imagined worlds. These stories can range from high fantasy, like The Lord of the Rings, to urban fantasy, where magic exists alongside the modern world. It lets readers escape into realms where the impossible becomes possible.

Romance novels focus on love stories and the emotional journey between two or more characters. They can range from historical romance (Pride and Prejudice) to contemporary (The Notebook) and even mix with other genres, like paranormal romance.

Centered around solving a crime or unraveling a puzzle, mystery novels keep readers on edge with clues, red herrings, and shocking reveals. Sherlock Holmes is a classic detective story, while Gone Girl represents modern psychological twists.

Thrillers are intense, fast-paced novels that often involve danger, high stakes, and suspense. They can range from spy thrillers like The Bourne Identity to psychological thrillers like The Girl on the Train, designed to keep readers on the edge of their seats.

Sci-Fi explores futuristic concepts like advanced technology, space exploration, time travel, and extraterrestrial life. These stories often challenge the boundaries of human imagination while reflecting on social, ethical, or philosophical issues. Dune and The Expanse are prime examples.
